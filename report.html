Estimating Risk

Determining the risks to your property

National Fire Management Decision Making

The US Forest Service Research branch quantifies risk across the western US.These maps are at too coarse a resolution (100-300 meters) for estimating fire risk to individual properties, however they show how fire can spread across the landscape onto your private property.


Why use drones?

The existing forest lidar data products can be anywhere from one to more than 10 years old. Changes to the forest structure, particularly on your private property may not be up-to-date with recent thinning projects. By flying drones, we can survey a neighborhood in a few hours, and develop datasets which are 100 to 1,000 times denser than those with aerial lidar in under a day. These new data can be compared to older lidar datasets, or to older drone datasets. By comparing changes overtime, we can show improvements from recent thinning efforts which may make your home within specification for recommended Firewise spacing.

How to use the Map

Clicking on the Map feature will open up a webmap with information pre-loaded about the Jemez Mountain WUI. You can use the search bar to enter your address or community name, and the map will zoom in over your property.

Around every structure that was mapped by Microsoft, or by our team (by hand), you can see the buffered rings which represent the Firewise suggested buffers.

We have calculated the number of tree tops visible in the aerial lidar or drone data, their canopy spacing, and the percent canopy cover of the area around the strucure in that zone.
